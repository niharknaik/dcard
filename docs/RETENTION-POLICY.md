# DCard — Data Retention Policy

Product: **DCard**, a product of **COPG Global**. Market: **India**. Surfaces: Laravel 12 API + Filament admin (`backend/`), Next.js web (`web/`), React Native Android app (`mobile/`). Payments: **Razorpay**.

This policy defines **how long DCard keeps each category of personal data, the trigger that starts the retention clock, and what happens to data on account deletion** (hard-deleted vs anonymized vs retained). It implements the DPDP-Act retention/erasure obligations tracked in [`COMPLIANCE.md`](./COMPLIANCE.md) §1 and §3, and is the policy referenced by the public [`/data-deletion`](../web/app/data-deletion/page.tsx) page.

> **Not legal advice.** This is operational guidance for the DCard team. Statutory retention periods (especially tax/GST record-keeping) must be confirmed with a Chartered Accountant / legal counsel before relying on them. Items marked **"confirm with CA"** or **"confirm statutory period"** are placeholders pending professional confirmation.

## 1. Scope

This policy covers all **personal data** processed by DCard as a Data Fiduciary, across the API/database, object storage (Cloudflare R2), logs, error monitoring (Sentry), and email/queue systems. It applies to:

- **Account holders** (registered users of the app/web).
- **Card visitors / data principals** whose data is captured via the public lead form.
- Operational and analytics data generated by use of the service.

It does **not** cover card data / PAN — DCard stores **no card data**; payment instruments are handled entirely by Razorpay (see [`COMPLIANCE.md`](./COMPLIANCE.md) §2).

The guiding principle is **DPDP storage limitation**: personal data is retained only as long as necessary for the stated purpose or as required by law, then erased or anonymized.

## 2. Data categories, retention period & trigger

| # | Data category | Examples / fields | Storage | Retention period | Retention trigger |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Account / profile data** | name, email, phone, hashed password, avatar, `consent_accepted_at`, `last_login_at/ip` | `users` table | Life of the account; erased on account deletion | Account deletion or consent withdrawal |
| 2 | **Cards & card content** | card fields, designation, company, contact, social links, services | `cards` and related tables | Life of the account; deleted with the card or on account deletion | Card deletion / account deletion |
| 3 | **Uploaded media** | avatar, card photos, portfolio images | Cloudflare R2 (private + signed URLs) | Life of the related card/account | Card/account deletion |
| 4 | **Leads (visitor-provided)** | visitor name, email, phone, message, `source`, `ip_address` | `leads` table (soft-deletable) | **Owned and controlled by the card owner.** Kept while the owning card/account exists; deleted/anonymized on account deletion | Card owner deletes lead, or owner's account deletion |
| 5 | **Portfolio items** | portfolio entries + images | `portfolio_items` + R2 | Life of the related card/account | Card/account deletion |
| 6 | **Subscriptions** | plan, status, `starts_at`/`ends_at`, `auto_renew` | `subscriptions` table | Retained while linked to payment/invoice records (financial audit trail), then erased per category 8 rules | Linked to invoice retention (see #8) |
| 7 | **Payment records** | `transaction_id`, `razorpay_order_id`, `amount`, `currency`, `status`, `paid_at`, `refunded_at`. **No card data / PAN.** | `payments` table | **Statutory tax/accounting period — retained even after account deletion.** | Statutory financial record-keeping |
| 8 | **Invoices** | invoice number, line items, tax fields, customer details on the invoice | `payments` + generated invoice PDFs | **Retained for the statutory GST/tax record-keeping period — approximately 8 years for Indian tax records (confirm with CA).** Not deleted on account deletion. | Statutory GST/tax retention |
| 9 | **Reward wallet & transactions** | balance, point transactions, `source`, `description` | `reward_wallets`, `reward_transactions` | Life of the account; deleted/anonymized on account deletion. Any portion tied to a paid redemption that has a financial record follows #7/#8 | Account deletion |
| 10 | **Referrals** | referral code, referrer/referred link, points awarded, status | `referrals` | Life of the account; on deletion the deleted user's link is removed/anonymized so the counterparty's record stays consistent | Account deletion |
| 11 | **Analytics events** | `visitor_hash`, `ip_address`, `user_agent`, `referrer`, `country`, event type | `analytics_events` (immutable, `created_at` only) | Raw events retained for a **rolling operational window — confirm statutory period (suggested 12–24 months)**, then **aggregated/anonymized** into daily rollups | Age of the event row / aggregation job |
| 12 | **Aggregated analytics** | daily per-card view/lead counts | `card_analytics_daily` | Retained as **anonymized/aggregated** statistics (no longer personal data); not tied to deletion | Not personal data after aggregation |
| 13 | **Activity / audit logs** | admin actions, security-relevant events | `activity_logs` | Retained for a **security/audit window — confirm period (suggested 12 months)**; scrub or anonymize personal identifiers where possible | Age of the log entry |
| 14 | **Application & access logs** | request logs, error traces (Sentry), queue logs | server logs / Sentry | Short rolling window per log-management policy (see [`LAUNCH-CHECKLIST.md`](./LAUNCH-CHECKLIST.md) §2); **must not contain PII or payment payloads** | Log rotation schedule |
| 15 | **Notifications / transactional email metadata** | in-app notifications, send logs | `notifications`, mail logs | Life of the account / short log window | Account deletion / log rotation |
| 16 | **Consent records** | `consent_accepted_at` (account), lead-form consent | `users`, lead capture | Retained as evidence of consent for as long as the related data is held, then erased | Erasure of the related data |

> **Note on the current implementation.** Account deletion is implemented as a **soft delete** (`UserService::deleteAccount` → `SoftDeletes`). Soft-deleted rows still hold personal data and therefore do **not yet** satisfy DPDP erasure on their own. To meet this policy, a follow-up job must **hard-delete or anonymize** soft-deleted accounts and their related data after the processing window, while preserving the financial records in categories 7–8. Tracked as an action item below.

## 3. On account deletion — deleted vs anonymized vs retained

This section is the authoritative mapping behind the public [`/data-deletion`](../web/app/data-deletion/page.tsx) page. It must stay consistent with that page (which states: deletion processed within **30 days**; public cards offline immediately; tax/invoice records retained).

### Hard-deleted (removed entirely)
- Account profile: name, email, phone, hashed password, avatar.
- Cards and all card content; **public cards/links/QR codes stop working immediately** on confirmation.
- Uploaded media (avatars, card photos, portfolio images) in R2.
- Leads owned by the user's cards (visitor-provided data the user controlled).
- Reward wallet and reward transactions not tied to a financial record.
- In-app notifications.

### Anonymized (retained without identifying the person)
- **Analytics**: raw events are aggregated/anonymized into daily rollups; the aggregates are non-personal statistics and are kept.
- **Referrals**: the deleted user's identity is removed/anonymized from referral records so the counterparty's history remains consistent.
- **Audit logs**: personal identifiers scrubbed where the log entry must be retained for security.

### Retained (required by law — survives account deletion)
- **Payment and invoice records** (transaction reference, amount, plan, dates, tax fields) needed for **GST/tax, accounting and financial-compliance** — retained for the statutory period (**~8 years, confirm with CA**).
- Any records DCard must keep to **comply with a legal obligation, resolve a dispute, or prevent/detect fraud**, for as long as that purpose requires.

**Timeline:** public cards removed immediately on confirmation; full deletion/anonymization of backend data completed **within 30 days** of confirmation or identity verification, except the legally retained categories above.

## 4. Withdrawal of consent

DPDP requires that withdrawing consent is **as easy as giving it** (see [`COMPLIANCE.md`](./COMPLIANCE.md) §1).

- A user may **withdraw consent** via in-app/web account settings or by emailing **info@copg.in**.
- On withdrawal, DCard **stops processing** the affected personal data and, where withdrawal removes the lawful basis for holding it, treats the data per the **erasure** rules in §3 (subject to the legal-retention carve-outs).
- Withdrawal does **not** force deletion of records DCard is **legally required** to keep (e.g. invoices), and this is disclosed to the user.
- **Card visitors** who submitted a lead may request withdrawal/erasure; because leads are **owned by the card owner**, DCard will route/forward such requests to the relevant card owner and act as processor where applicable, while honoring direct erasure requests it can fulfill.

## 5. Data principal rights → endpoints

| Right (DPDP) | How it is satisfied | Endpoint / mechanism |
|---|---|---|
| **Access / export** | User can export all data held about them | `GET /v1/account/export` (`ProfileController@export`) — returns profile, cards, subscriptions, payments, reward wallet, referrals |
| **Correction** | User edits their profile/card data | `PUT /v1/profile` (`ProfileController@update`) + card edit endpoints |
| **Erasure** | User deletes account + public deletion path | `DELETE /v1/account` (`ProfileController@destroy`) and the public [`/data-deletion`](../web/app/data-deletion/page.tsx) page / email to info@copg.in |
| **Grievance / nominate** | Contact the Grievance Officer | **info@copg.in** (published on `/privacy`, the app, and the store listing) |
| **Consent withdrawal** | See §4 | Account settings / email |

## 6. Logging & data-minimization standing rules

- **Never log PII** (names, emails, phones) or **payment payloads** in application logs or Sentry (see [`COMPLIANCE.md`](./COMPLIANCE.md) §2 and [`LAUNCH-CHECKLIST.md`](./LAUNCH-CHECKLIST.md) §2).
- **Strip EXIF/geolocation** from uploaded photos.
- Collect only fields actually used; avoid optional fields with no purpose.
